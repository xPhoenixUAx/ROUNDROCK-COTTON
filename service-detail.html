Development & Support

Development and support work best when delivery does not stop at handoff. The same team that understands the implementation logic should also be able to support launch readiness, fixes, integrations, and ongoing improvement after the first version goes live.

For ROUNDROCK COTTON, this means connecting frontend and backend delivery, technical QA, release preparation, and retained support into one accountable production workflow. Where the project requires it, we also work with widely used tools and services such as Google Analytics 4, Google Tag Manager, Meta Pixel, CRM systems, form tools, email platforms, content systems, and other established business services that need to be connected into the website properly.

What this service solves

Digital projects often break down between handoff points: design is approved, implementation drifts, integrations are added too late, launch gets rushed, and support becomes reactive. We solve for continuity across build, release, and the updates that follow so the website stays operational, maintainable, and easier to improve over time.

Real business value

The business value is steadier delivery, fewer technical surprises at launch, faster follow-up fixes, cleaner integration with important marketing and business tools, and a much stronger base for ongoing product improvement after the first release.

What is included

A connected implementation and support layer rather than isolated technical help.

Website implementation

We handle structured build work for new websites, redesign rollouts, landing pages, and production delivery based on approved design direction and agreed business requirements.

Frontend delivery

We build responsive interfaces, reusable components, interaction handling, and production-ready page structures that support both presentation quality and practical day-to-day use.

Backend and integrations

We set up forms, content flows, custom logic, and integrations with known systems such as Google Analytics 4, Google Tag Manager, Meta Pixel, CRM tools, email services, booking tools, and other business platforms where they are relevant to the project.

QA and release support

We handle cross-device review, issue resolution, content checks, form testing, and launch preparation so the release is cleaner, more stable, and less reactive on day one.

Maintenance and updates

We support post-launch fixes, content changes, technical refinements, and small-scale improvements through an ongoing support path that helps the website stay current instead of gradually falling behind.

Technical partner continuity

We stay involved as a steady technical partner after release, so there is a clear route for fixes, improvements, new needs, and platform updates instead of an abrupt drop in support.

Approach

We move through five practical stages so build quality, launch readiness, and support continuity stay aligned.

01

Scope and technical review

Review the current state of the product, technical constraints, launch expectations, and what needs immediate attention first.

02

Implementation plan

Define build priorities, templates, functionality, and support responsibilities before the heavier delivery phase starts.

03

Build and fix phase

Deliver the required frontend, backend, and integration work with clear checkpoints and issue resolution along the way.

04

Release readiness

Review cross-device behavior, forms, content, and operational details before a new release or update goes live.

05

Ongoing support

Stay involved for fixes, updates, prioritised improvements, and technical continuity after the initial delivery window.
