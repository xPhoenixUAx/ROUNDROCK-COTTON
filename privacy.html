Privacy Policy

Effective date: January 1, 2026. Last updated: January 1, 2026. This policy applies under the GDPR and applicable Czech and EU data protection legislation.

Summary

  • We collect business, contact, technical, usage, and service-related data when you interact with us.
  • We use personal data to deliver services, manage communication, improve performance, and meet legal obligations.
  • We do not sell personal data.
  • You have GDPR-related rights including access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, objection, and withdrawal of consent.

01. Who We Are

This website is operated by ROUNDROCK COTTON s.r.o., a company registered in the Czech Republic, with its registered office at Lidická 700/19, 602 00 Brno, Czech Republic.

We provide digital marketing, advertising, and related business support services to companies across Europe and internationally.

For the purposes of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (“GDPR”) and applicable Czech and EU data protection legislation, ROUNDROCK COTTON s.r.o. acts as the data controller in respect of personal data collected through this website.

We are committed to protecting the personal data of our website visitors, prospective clients, and business partners. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

02. Data We Collect

We may collect personal data when you interact with our website, submit a form, communicate with us, or engage our services.

The types of data we may collect include identity data such as name, surname, company name, and job title; contact data such as email address, phone number, and business address; financial data such as invoicing details, VAT numbers, and billing-related information where applicable; technical data such as IP address, browser type, device information, time zone, and system data; usage data such as pages visited, time on site, navigation paths, and interactions; marketing data such as preferences for receiving communications and campaign-related interactions; and business data shared in relation to services, including marketing goals, budgets, and project scope.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data. If such data is provided, it will be deleted where appropriate.

03. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data to respond to inquiries and contact requests, provide and improve our services, manage client relationships and communications, process invoices and business transactions, analyse website performance and user behaviour, support marketing and advertising activities, ensure website security and prevent misuse, and comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

We do not use your data for unrelated purposes without informing you.

04. Legal Basis for Processing

We rely on the following legal bases: contract performance when processing is necessary to provide services; legitimate interests to improve services, ensure security, and operate our business; legal obligation to comply with applicable laws and regulations; and consent where required, including for marketing communications and cookies.

You may withdraw consent at any time.

05. Data Retention

We retain data only as long as necessary. Client and financial data may be retained for up to 7 years to meet legal requirements. Inquiry data may be retained for up to 2 years. Analytics data may be retained for up to 26 months. Marketing data may be retained until consent is withdrawn.

We may anonymise data for analytical purposes where appropriate.

06. Sharing Your Data

We do not sell your personal data.

We may share data with service providers such as hosting providers, CRM systems, and email tools; advertising platforms including Google and Meta; professional advisers such as legal and accounting advisers; and authorities where required by law.

All third parties are required to handle data securely and lawfully.

07. International Transfers

Some providers, including Google and Meta, may process data outside the EEA.

Where this occurs, we use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), approved data transfer mechanisms, and GDPR-compliant contractual protections.

08. Your Rights

You have the right to access your data, correct inaccurate data, request deletion, restrict processing, receive your data in a portable format, object to processing, and withdraw consent.

To exercise your rights, contact us using the details below.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the competent data protection authority in the Czech Republic.

09. Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to improve website functionality, analyse usage, support marketing efforts, and measure campaign effectiveness.

For full details, please refer to our Cookie Policy.
